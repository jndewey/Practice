# Real Estate Support for Corporate Healthcare Transactions

Skill and reference materials for real estate counsel supporting large healthcare corporate transactions, including asset acquisitions, stock purchases, and mergers.

## Contents

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `real estate corporate healthcare support skill.md` | End-to-end guide covering all phases of real estate counsel's role in healthcare M&A transactions, from initial assessment through post-closing. |

## Key Topics Covered

### Phase 1: Transaction Structuring and Initial Assessment
- Understanding transaction structure (asset acquisition vs. stock purchase vs. merger)
- Healthcare-specific considerations (permitted uses, regulatory permits, zoning)
- Timing and lead-time items (surveys, title searches, estoppel certificates)
- Financing considerations (existing mortgages, mortgage recording taxes)

### Phase 2: Due Diligence
- Title and survey review (title company selection, insurance requirements by transaction type)
- Lease review (assignment provisions, change of control clauses)
- Landlord consent analysis (asset acquisitions vs. stock purchases/mergers)
- Related party leases and estoppel certificates

### Phase 3: Purchase Agreement Negotiations
- Representations and warranties for owned and leased property
- Permitted liens and encumbrances
- Pre-closing covenants
- Closing conditions and deliveries (title policies, surveys, landlord consents, SNDAs, FIRPTA)

### Phase 4: Finance Document Review
- Credit agreement review, mortgage and deed of trust principles
- Landlord lien waivers

### Phase 5: Pre-Closing and Closing
- Closing checklist management and third-party coordination
- Title-related closing documents and closing statement review

### Phase 6: Transfer Taxes and Recording Fees
- Fee transfer and leasehold transfer tax analysis
- Mortgage recording tax strategies (assignment vs. discharge)
