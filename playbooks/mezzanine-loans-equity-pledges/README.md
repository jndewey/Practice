# Mezzanine Loans & Equity Pledges

Comprehensive knowledge base and reference materials for mezzanine loan transactions in commercial real estate finance, covering the full lifecycle from structuring and documentation through foreclosure and secondary market sales.

## Contents

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `SKILL.md` | Core skill definition with conceptual overview of mezzanine loan structure, entity requirements, document packages, pledge mechanics, risks, loan-to-own strategies, and negotiation quick reference. |
| `pledge-agreement.md` | Detailed drafting guidance for pledge and security agreements. |
| `intercreditor.md` | Negotiation and review guidance for intercreditor agreements between mortgage and mezzanine lenders. |
| `ucc-collateral-docs.md` | Drafting guidance for UCC collateral documents (Article 8 opt-in amendments, certificates of LLC interests, control agreements, irrevocable proxies, acknowledgment letters, UCC-1 financing statements). |
| `foreclosure-remedies.md` | Guidance on UCC foreclosure of membership interests and available remedies. |
| `due-diligence-closing.md` | Due diligence and closing checklist for mezzanine loan transactions. |

## Key Topics Covered

### Core Concepts
- Mezzanine loan structure (equity collateral, structural subordination, higher interest rate)
- Capital stack configurations (single mezzanine, senior/junior mezzanine, multiple subordinate layers)
- Why mezzanine instead of a second mortgage (bankruptcy remoteness, lien priority preservation)
- LTV and the equity gap (mortgage at 60-70%, combined LTV at 80-90%)
- Standard two-SPE entity structure (mezzanine borrower owns mortgage borrower)

### Document Package
- Pledge and Security Agreement
- Promissory Note and Mezzanine Loan Agreement
- Nonrecourse Carveout Guaranty and Environmental Indemnity
- Subordination of Property Management Agreement
- Intercreditor Agreement
- Ancillary UCC Collateral Documents (Article 8 opt-in, certificates, control agreements, proxies)

### Pledge Mechanics
- Creation and perfection under UCC (general intangibles vs. investment property/securities)
- Perfection by control vs. filing (control takes priority)
- Certificated vs. uncertificated interests
- Protected purchaser status through Article 8 opt-in

### Risks and Strategies
- Risks for mezzanine borrowers (fast foreclosure, dual approvals, loan-to-own exposure)
- Risks for mezzanine lenders (first loss position, lack of property control, collateral dilution)
- Risks for mortgage lenders (DSCR drain, guarantor erosion, post-foreclosure bankruptcy risk)
- Loan-to-own strategy mechanics and when it works

### Negotiation Quick Reference
- Priorities when representing mezzanine lenders, mezzanine borrowers, or mortgage lenders
- Secondary market considerations (assignment documents, seller representations, borrower consent rights)
