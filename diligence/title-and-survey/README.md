# Title Insurance and Survey Review

Comprehensive skill for reviewing title commitments and ALTA/NSPS surveys in commercial real estate loan transactions from the lender's perspective.

## Contents

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `TITLE_AND_SURVEY_REVIEW_SKILL.md` | Full title and survey review skill covering commitment annotation, analytical memorandum preparation, Florida endorsement reference, standard lender requirements, and special situation guidance. |

## Key Topics Covered

### Part A: Commitment Annotation Instructions
- FreeText annotation format and style for title commitments
- Standard annotation checks (Schedule A, Schedule B-I/B-II)
- Endorsement request formatting
- Pro forma policy requests
- Single parcel vs. multi-parcel collateral scope

### Part B: Title and Survey Review Memorandum
- Structured output format for internal analytical memos
- Schedule A review checklist (effective date, insurance amount, vesting, legal description)
- Schedule B-I requirements analysis (payoffs, entity authority, taxes, UCC searches)
- Schedule B-II exceptions analysis (categorization, plottability, lender impact, required actions)
- Survey review protocol (certification, legal description comparison, physical matters, plotted exceptions)
- Table A items for ALTA/NSPS surveys

### Part C: Florida Endorsement Reference
- Endorsements prohibited in Florida (Access, Location, Zoning, Non-Imputation, and others)
- Endorsements available in Florida (FL Form 9, Survey, ALTA 8.1, Variable Rate, Contiguity, Navigational Servitude)
- Transaction-specific endorsements (Construction Loan Update, Future Advance, Condominium, PUD, Leasehold, Aggregation)
- Florida endorsement selection decision tree

### Parts D-I: Additional Guidance
- Standard lender title requirements (GAP coverage, tax exceptions, lease/possession, mechanics liens)
- Special situations (construction loans, multiple parcels, waterfront property, condominiums, leaseholds, PUDs)
- Estoppel letter requirements and templates
- Red flags requiring immediate escalation
- Document request checklists
- Florida-specific quick reference (documentary stamps, intangible tax, submerged lands, homestead)
